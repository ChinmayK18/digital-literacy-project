# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting college students and young adults in India. In a typical scenario, a student receives a phone call or message from someone pretending to be a bank representative or online seller. The fraudster claims that the student must verify their account details or receive a refund through a UPI request. The victim is then asked to approve a payment request or share an OTP.

Believing the request to be genuine, the student unknowingly authorizes the transaction. As a result, money is instantly transferred from the victim’s bank account to the fraudster’s account. Since UPI transactions are fast and irreversible in many cases, recovering the lost money becomes difficult.

Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use digital payment apps and may not always verify unknown requests carefully. Such fraud can lead to financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal banking information.

This case study highlights the importance of verifying payment requests, avoiding sharing OTPs, and checking sender authenticity before approving any transaction. Awareness and careful digital behaviour can significantly reduce the risk of becoming a victim of UPI fraud.
